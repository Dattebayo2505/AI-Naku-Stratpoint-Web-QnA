Cloud Adoption in Financial Services: Why Monitoring is the Key to Success

The financial services industry is undergoing a significant transformation, and Cloud adoption is becoming increasingly vital for innovation and growth. However, navigating the complexities of Cloud adoption and migration requires a strong monitoring strategy. While moving to the Cloud offers scalability, cost efficiency, and innovation, effective monitoring is the key to a successful transition.  

# **Financial Services are turning to the Cloud**

Cloud adoption in the financial services industry is gaining momentum, with industry forecasts predicting a surge in spending. According to IDC, banking, software, and information services will account for [$326 billion in public Cloud investments by 2027](https://financialit.net/blog/financialservices-cloudtechnology/how-financial-services-sector-moving-cloud-and-what-it-means). While many financial firms see Cloud as crucial for growth, some still hesitate to migrate core business applications, with only [32% of financial services firms](https://financialit.net/blog/financialservices-cloudtechnology/how-financial-services-sector-moving-cloud-and-what-it-means) moving their critical workloads to the Cloud.

# **Why Cloud adoption in Financial Services is slow**

While financial service institutions recognize the benefits of Cloud technology, several [obstacles](https://www.redhat.com/en/topics/financial-services/why-financial-services-are-slow-to-adopt-cloud#what-are-some-of-the-key-adoption-challenges-for-financial-services-leaving-legacy-systems) continue to slow down Cloud adoption.

* **Regulatory compliance** – The financial sector is highly regulated, with stringent requirements for data privacy and security.
* **Security risks** – Migrating sensitive data increases exposure to cyber threats. Without real-time monitoring, detecting breaches becomes more difficult.
* **Integration with legacy systems** – Many banks still rely on mainframe technology, making Cloud adoption complex and expensive.
* **Unrealized Cloud investments** – Many companies in the financial services industry are [hesitant to utilize Cloud investments at scale](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-digital/our-insights/three-big-moves-that-can-decide-a-financial-institutions-future-in-the-cloud), leading to inefficiencies, including wasted Cloud spend, operational inefficiencies, and missed business value.

# **Importance of Monitoring in Cloud Adoption**

As financial service institutions move to the Cloud, monitoring becomes a non-negotiable factor in ensuring success. Without effective monitoring, Cloud environments remain vulnerable to security threats, compliance failures, and performance bottlenecks.
